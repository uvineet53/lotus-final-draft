Malaysia, the multi-ethnic and multi-religious country in Southeast Asia. Known for its beaches, rainforest and a mix of Malay, Chinese, Indian and European cultural influences. India and Malaysia are also connected by various cultural and historical ties that date back to antiquity.As Malaysia is home to a strong concentration of Indian immigrants. Here you can experience.

THE PETRONAS TOWERS:-:- A pair of super tall skyscrapers also known as twin towers. Ithas awesome view over Kuala lumpur city. PETRONAS Twin Towers are the tallest twin towers in the world, and its status has remained unchallenged since 1996.The Petronas Towers are a major landmark of Kuala Lumpur, along with the nearby Kuala Lumpur Tower and Merdeka 118, and are visible in many places.

BATU CAVES:- Batu Caves is a mogote that has a series of caves and cave temples in Gombak, Selangor, Malaysia. One of the countries biggest attractions and a place of pilgrimage for Tamil Hindus. That has series of caves and cave temples in Malaysia.

TAMAN NEGARA:- The National Park of Peninsular Malaysia (Taman Negara), originally was gazetted as Gunung Tahan Here you should Visit for its unmatched flora and fauna or to get the hang of the original inhabitants of Malaysia, the Orang Asli tribe. You get to walk on the longest canopy walkway, experience trekking under the rainforest canopy, fishing, camping, observing wildlife, and so much more.

KL BIRD PARK:-Kuala Lumpur Bird Park Its located adjacent to the Lake Gardens within a KL green lung in Bukit Aman, close to the National Mosque and Royal Malaysian Police Museum. Major attractions of the Kuala Lumpur Bird Park are Hornbills, parrots, the egg incubation area, flamingo pond, bird gallery, and others. A visit to the KL Bird Park is a must if one wants to enjoy the beauty of nature with colorful birds around them.

AQUARIA KLCC:- Aquaria KLCC is a state-of-the-art aquarium located in the heart of the city, beneath the Kuala Lumpur Convention Centre and a stone's throw from the iconic Aquaria KLCC houses close to 5000 species of marine and terrestrial animals. They have different packages that will help you interact intimately with the animals. They have an underwater tunnel, Living Ocean, which houses five species of sharks and turtles! That attracts tourists.

BAKO NATIONAL PARK:- Bako is one of the smallest national parks in Sarawak. However, it features multiple biome With its rainforest, abundant wildlife, jungle streams, waterfalls, unique flora and fauna, secluded beaches, and trekking trails, Bako National Park offers visitors an excellent introduction to the rainforest of Borneo.

LANGKAWI SKY BRIDGE:- Langkawi Sky Bridge is a 125-metre curved pedestrian cable-stayed bridge in Malaysia. The bridge deck is 2,170 ft above sea level at the peak of Gunung Mat Cincang on Pulau Langkawi, the main island of the Langkawi archipelago in Kedah. Witness panoramic views due to the beautiful curved architecture of the bridge that lets you catch every detail. Experience the rush of adrenaline by standing on the transparent floor of the bridge to experience hauntingly beautiful heights as you look beneath.
